Background

Why this matters

Heart disease kills 17.9 million people per year — 31% of all global deaths. Most patients are not identified as high risk until after a serious cardiac event. This project asks: can routine self-reported lifestyle data identify high-risk individuals before that happens?

Methodology

How we built the models

A complete ML pipeline — real CDC data, encoding, scaling, GridSearchCV tuning, and rigorous evaluation on a held-out test set.

01📥Sample2,059 at real 8.6% disease rate
02🔍EDACharts · stats · 0 missing
03🔠EncodeOrdinal · binary · one-hot
04✂Split 80/201,647 train · 412 test
05⚖ScaleStandardScaler (train only)
06🎯GridSearchF1 · 5-fold · balanced
Why class_weight='balanced'? With 91.4% no disease, a model predicting "no disease" for everyone gets 91.4% accuracy — while being completely useless. class_weight=balanced forces the algorithm to weight disease patients proportionally higher.
Why keep the imbalance instead of forcing 50/50? A forced 50/50 split misrepresents reality. Preserving the real 8.6% rate and handling it with class_weight is the academically correct approach for imbalanced medical classification.
No data leakage: StandardScaler is fitted on training data ONLY. The same parameters are then applied to the test set. Fitting on all data first produces falsely optimistic results.

Ethics

Responsible AI in healthcare

A model that makes health predictions carries serious responsibilities. We address five key ethical dimensions before any clinical deployment could be considered.

⚖
Bias & Fairness
The dataset covers US respondents and includes race as a feature. Disparities in healthcare access across racial and socioeconomic groups may cause the model to perform differently for different subpopulations. Subgroup evaluation is required before any deployment (Barocas et al., 2019).
📋
Self-Reported Data Limitations
Unlike clinical datasets, BRFSS uses telephone surveys. Respondents may under-report behaviours, have undiagnosed conditions, or recall inaccurately. This inherent noise limits model accuracy — a well-understood limitation of population health surveys.
🚨
False Negatives Are Dangerous
A false negative — predicting no disease when disease is present — could delay treatment. This is why we optimise for F1-score, use class_weight=balanced, and report disease recall separately. The threshold can be lowered to catch more sick patients.
🔒
Privacy — GDPR & HIPAA
Any real deployment handles protected health data subject to EU GDPR (right to explanation, data minimisation) and US HIPAA (access controls, audit trails). The physician always makes the final call — this model is decision support only.
